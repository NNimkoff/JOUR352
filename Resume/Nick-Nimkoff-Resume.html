About Me


Nicholas "Nick" Nimkoff is 21 year old a Journalism student at UMD, currently double majoring in Film Studies. He is from Orlando, FL and graduated from Lake Highland Prepatory School in 2018. His currently living in the College Park, MD area where the University of Maryland is located, very close to Washington D.C. He has had a passion for film since a young age and follows the film industry closely, keeping up with whatever the latest in entertainment may be. He is also a dedicated journalist, whether through podcast or blog posts Nick loves to write about film and media. He has grand ambitions to join the entertainment journalism industry and following in the footsteps of the great Rodger Ebert as his inspiration. He has spent years informing himself on film on a global scale, seeing any movie he could from every country he could to help inform him on the state of the entire industry. He was an active member of his high school's student newspaper, Twice Told Tale, and accompanying student magazine, By Any Other Name, where he honed his skills as a journalist and worked his way up from Co-Editor to Director of Photography to Co-Editor in Cheif. He has written for many of UMD's publications, including the Diamondback, writing stories about the community and the entertainment that can be found all around campus. He also enjoys writing about video games, amusement parks, music, art, television and comic book culture. He is currently in his junior year and plans to graduate Spring 2022.


Experience



I have been working concessions and usher duties at my local Regal theatre for approximately 2 years. At Regal, I have learned customer service skills that have been invaluable to me in helping communicate with others on a commercial level. It also immensely helped in understanding the customer side of the movie going experience by being able to work hands on in the industry. I have been brought back over two consecutive summers to work with the team after time off of work for school.


I have worked with my parents company for some time, doing various tasks in and around the office. I was able to learn essential business skills from my parents this way and informed myself about what it is like to work in an office.


At the Diamondback I wrote stories about campus life, focusing on arts and entertainment during the 2020 coronavirus pandemic. I was able to hone my craft here and get a sense for what it would be like to work with editors in a somewhat professional news environment.


At Unwind I was able to explore a semi-professional outlet for reviews, a format I am still very fond of. It helped me to understand the world of professional reviewers and the amount of work that goes into every single section of the paper.


WMUC provided me with an opportunity to further explore writing for the community while focusing on music. I wrote a piece on the coronavirus's effect on the student body's musicians and the at-home concerts they were able to put on while being in a strange, new evironment.
